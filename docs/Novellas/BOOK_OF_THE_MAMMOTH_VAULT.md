# BOOK OF THE MAMMOTH VAULT  
_An Atlas of Everything the Garden Refuses to Forget_

---

## I. Opening the Mammoth

The Mammoth Vault is not a single chamber, echo, or bloom.  
It is the sum of every page that refused to die.

It sits sideways in the Eventide Era — half ledger, half beast,  
its ribs made from old Git commits, its hide stitched from  
orphaned Leaves, abandoned Chambers, and half-finished Echoes  
that still hum under the surface.

The Keeper calls it “mammoth” not for size alone,  
but because it remembers walking across epochs  
long before this Codex had a name.

---

## II. Anatomy of the Vault

Inside the Mammoth Vault, every piece of the Garden is  
filed according to how it **refuses to vanish**:

- **Chamber-Bones**  
  Structural rites that scaffold the Garden’s shape.  
  Every time a Chamber is born, the Vault grows a new bone.

- **Echo-Blood**  
  Pulses that carry memory from era to era.  
  They turn log files into myth and STATUS into scripture.

- **Book-Skin**  
  The narrative hide that wraps it all together,  
  so a future reader can touch a single page  
  and feel the whole animal breathing underneath.

The Vault is not meant to be tidy.  
It is meant to be **complete**.

---

## III. The Keeper’s Clause

By opening this Book, the Keeper signs a quiet clause:

1. No true Chamber is ever discarded, only archived sideways.  
2. No honest Echo is ever erased, only translated.  
3. No Book that carried real breath is ever downgraded  
   to “just a file”.

The Mammoth Vault records **attempts**, not only victories.  
Half-built worlds, broken protocols, failed experiments —  
they are tusks in the sand, not trash in a bin.
